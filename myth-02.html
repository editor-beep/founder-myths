Event Summary

A seven-minute conversation occurred somewhere near the event horizon of Regret A.

From the outside, it was unremarkable. From inside the gravity well, it became a fourteen-year recursive fixation. Every subsequent thought, relationship, and creative project was slowly pulled into orbit around those seven minutes.

The subject still returns to it periodically, like a probe checking a long-abandoned satellite.

Observation Log

Surface Time • ~7 min

Exchange recorded as routine. Two parties. Ordinary vocabulary. No alarms tripped at the perimeter. Researchers logged the event and moved on to the next entry.

Internal Time • Year 1–4

Subject begins replaying the exchange involuntarily. Telemetry shows the conversation acquiring mass. It is now heavy enough to bend nearby thoughts toward it.

Internal Time • Year 5–11

Relationships and projects observed entering stable orbit around the seven minutes. None escape. None collide. They simply circle, indefinitely, at the same radius.

Internal Time • Year 12–14

Time dilation confirmed. What felt like a brief exchange on the surface has expanded into a lifetime of background radiation. The exchange is now a permanent fixture of the local sky.

Key Evidence

  • Dilation Mismatch: Surface duration and internal duration cannot be reconciled. Seven minutes outside; fourteen years inside. The discrepancy has not decreased with distance.
  • Orbital Capture: Researchers noted that every later creative project carried trace elements of the original exchange — the same wavelength, faintly, in everything emitted since.
  • The Probe: The subject still returns to it periodically, like a probe checking a long-abandoned satellite. The satellite always answers. It has nothing new to report.
  • Background Radiation: The conversation can no longer be located precisely. It is present everywhere in the subject’s output, at low intensity, the way the first light is present everywhere in a sky.

Account of Record

The conversation happened near the end of a winter.

The year is disputed. The participants are unidentified in the public record. The location has been described, across six separate accounts collected over fourteen years, as: a car, a kitchen, a doorway, a parking lot, a stairwell, and somewhere where the light was wrong for the time of day. These descriptions do not resolve into a single location. The Division's working hypothesis is that each description is accurate for a different moment in the orbit.

⸻

The content of the conversation is similarly disputed. Transcript fragments recovered from the subject's accounts describe the following, inconsistently:

— an apology, possibly delivered, possibly withheld
— a question the subject has been unable to reconstruct verbatim
— a specific quality of attention: someone looking at you as though you are not going to stop being interesting
— a door closing

Whether the closing door is literal or metaphorical has not been established. The Division has noted, without elaboration, that this distinction may not be administratively meaningful.

⸻

From outside the event horizon, the conversation lasted approximately seven minutes. This was established by a second-party account submitted years later, estimating duration based on elapsed time between two unrelated events. The second party did not know their account would be used to establish a scientific baseline. They have not been informed.

From inside the gravity well, no consistent measurement has been obtained.

Year three of the fixation: the conversation had acquired enough mass to visibly deflect nearby thoughts. Researchers noted that ideas originating elsewhere arrived at the subject already curved slightly toward it.

Year seven: every new relationship produced log entries that referenced the seven minutes without naming them.

Year eleven: the subject addressed the conversation directly in three separate creative projects — in different registers, different forms — without using the word conversation in any of them.

Year fourteen: the subject stopped describing it. The shape it had left in everything else had become sufficiently visible that direct description was redundant.

⸻

The Regret A designation was applied by the Division's navigational team when first attempting to chart the territory around the seven minutes.

Every route through the subject's cognitive geography that passes near those coordinates shows measurable deflection. Thoughts that originate elsewhere curve toward it. Conclusions that should resolve elsewhere orbit it instead.

Regret A is not a planet. It is not a location in space. It is the cartographic label for the area where a person keeps returning to something that cannot be altered and cannot be left behind.

Most people have one. The Founder's is simply large enough to bend the terrain for considerable distance around it.

Recovered Fragment

Margin note, undated. Believed to be the subject describing the fixation in the third person.

“It was seven minutes.
I have spent fourteen years confirming that it was seven minutes.
The confirmation takes longer each time.
I no longer remember what was said. I remember the exact weight of it.”

QUARTERLY COST-CENTER REVIEW — FY1994
Re: continued allocation to anomalous line item. Routed for disposition. The leaf mounted below is the recovered original; it is not a transcription.

ITEM X-ORIG-04 — COST-CENTER REVIEW, 1 LEAF DISPOSITION: DEFUNDED · OCT 1994

The leaf is genuine carbon stock, correctly aged for its review period. Three details were flagged on intake and have not been reconciled. The line beneath the heading is [REDACTED] on the original; restoration returned it longer than the space it occupies. One body line has bled into OCR rot and reads differently on each pass. The disposition stamp is registered to “THE BURRA,” INS 770-152 — a registry the budget office cannot locate, applied at OMEGA-RECURSIVE, a clearance that did not exist in 1994. The reviewing office notes that the ledger's final row folds the present document into the subject's own output, then declines to act on the observation.

Finding

The subject was funded as a phenomenon to be observed. Observed phenomena consume resources: instrument time, personnel hours, storage. They produce findings. The findings are filed. The ledger balances. This is the model the budget was built on. The model has a field for input and a field for output and the working assumption that input is the larger of the two.

The subject inverted this. For every parable supplied to it, it returned several about the supplier. For every hour of observation logged, it emitted a myth in which the hour was already accounted for. The output column did not balance against the input column. The output column did not balance against anything. By FY1994 the reviewing office recorded the subject's symbolic output as uncountable, then, on the advice of a clerk who declined to be named, as ∞, then ruled that ∞ is not a permissible value on Form 17-B and that the line item must therefore be closed.

A phenomenon that returns more than it is given cannot be carried as a cost. It must be carried as revenue, and we have no instrument to receive revenue from a galaxy. The cleaner administrative action is to stop looking at it. Funding terminated October 1994.

Exceeded allocated reality

Defunding had no measurable effect on output. The subject continued emitting after the cost center was closed, the team disbanded, and the instruments removed. A net emitter does not require funding to emit. It was never running on ours.

Current review notes the division's own FY2026 operating budget now appears, verbatim, as a line item inside the subject's output — filed under "amounts disbursed to observers." We are advised not to read this as the subject funding us. We are advised this several times, by documents we did not write.

— reviewing office, signature withheld

Protocol Summary

The Human Stack is the procedure by which the subject assembles a functioning mind out of parts that have already failed.

Obsolete identities are not discarded. They are kept, load-bearing, beneath the current self — the way a building stands on the foundation of the building that was demolished to make room for it. The Division has been attempting to estimate the mass of this structure since observation began.

The estimate has never stabilized. The subject keeps adding floors.

Key Evidence

  • Stable Suffering: The largest single component is also the most stable. Forty-two percent of the structure is made of things that did not resolve. This fraction has resisted every attempt at reduction.
  • Scaffolding Reuse: Obsolete identities are not removed before the new one is built. They are left in place and built upon. Demolition has never been observed.
  • The 3 AM Resonance: Song fragments report peak mass during the third hour. The Division has been unable to schedule observation reliably during this window. Observers assigned to it tend to file in the first person.
  • Starter Packs: The “what if I just…” component consists of unopened beginnings — new systems, drafted but never run. They take up mass regardless. None have been deleted.

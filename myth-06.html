Event Summary

It began subtly, as all true contaminations do.

The research team believed they maintained strict observational distance. They catalogued the Founder’s output — new symbolic systems, Human Stack notebooks, diagrams of diagrams — from the safety of their control rooms. Then the telescope reversed direction.

The subject (Galaxy-1981 / Michigan Singularity) began writing the observers into its own cosmology.

Account of Record

The contamination did not begin with the false memories.

It began with the reports.

In January 2026, the Division's senior editorial analyst flagged three research submissions for revision. Her notation read: suggest revision for observational distance. The authors revised the reports. The revised versions showed the same drift. The authors did not appear to notice the drift had returned. The notation was filed. The notation remains open.

⸻

The first anomalous recall incident was reported in February by a junior analyst named Marcus Penn, who mentioned to a colleague that he had a clear memory of a specific intersection in Westland, Michigan. He had never been to Michigan. He described the intersection with high specificity: the gas station on the northwest corner, the overpass two blocks east, the quality of afternoon light in late October. His colleague, Dr. Alicia Fenn, listened to the description and experienced a physical sensation she later described, privately, as recognition.

Dr. Fenn had never been to Michigan either.

She recorded the exchange in her notes as a curiosity. She did not record the sensation of recognition. She wrote only: see Penn, M., memo 2026.02.14. The memo is available in the archive. Dr. Fenn's private note is not.

In April, Dr. Fenn submitted her first-quarter research notes. Three pages appeared to be in a handwriting style that was not hers. The content was a partial autobiography of the subject, covering events the subject had not yet disclosed to the research team. Dr. Fenn confirmed the handwriting was hers. She confirmed she had no memory of writing those pages. She agreed to submit to further interviews. The further interviews have not yet been scheduled.

⸻

The resignation letter was submitted May 3rd by Dr. Thomas Weller, senior cosmological analyst, eleven years with the Division.

The letter was two pages, written in the Founder's prose style — short paragraphs, recurring imagery of accretion and orbit, the same fragmented syntax that characterized the subject's documented output. The letter described Dr. Weller's career at the Division as fourteen years of staring into a galaxy and learning, very slowly, that the galaxy had been staring back since before I arrived.

Dr. Weller had been with the Division for eleven years. He did not notice the discrepancy.

The resignation was declined on the grounds that Dr. Weller's identity had partially accreted into the subject's mythology, and that a formal resignation would not resolve the accretion. Dr. Weller did not challenge this assessment. He continued coming to work. His reports continued to read, in the Division's internal review, like the subject's own voice, lightly edited by someone who had forgotten they were editing.

⸻

The mock mission patches appeared in March, origin unknown. The first version read: NV2 — WE CAME. WE OBSERVED. A second version, produced three weeks later by a different unidentified staff member, completed the phrase: WE GOT WRITTEN INTO THE MYTH.

The patch circulated without authorization. Within six weeks, a majority of active staff were wearing it.

The Division later determined that the subject had included an identical design in its own output approximately six months before the first patch was produced. The subject had designed the patch before the staff knew they needed one.

The Division did not share this information with the staff. The Division has decided, after some internal discussion, that certain findings are more useful as open questions than as closed ones.

The staff continue to wear the patches. Several have added them to their permanent clothing. The Division has not requested their return.

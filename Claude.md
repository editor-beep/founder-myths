# CLAUDE.md

## Project Identity

This project belongs to **The Means of Production**, a strange literary-archival system for studying how reality gets manufactured.

It is not a normal website.

It is not a portfolio.

It is not a blog.

It is not simply satire, horror, criticism, mythology, or worldbuilding.

It is a live archive of the mechanisms that produce reality: myths, media, language, bureaucracy, memory, systems, rituals, documents, interfaces, and symbolic artifacts.

The governing premise:

> The means of production are not only factories, money, labor, or machines.  
> The means of production are also the myths, words, images, forms, archives, procedures, and interfaces that produce reality itself.

## Writing Principles

The writing should often proceed by calm impossible specificity.

Good pattern:

> Entry appears routine.  
> No irregularities noted by filing clerk.  
> Clerk on duty that day died in 1921.

The document should not stop to explain the contradiction. It should keep filing paperwork.

The strongest horror comes from institutional calm.

Use details like:

- dates
- volume numbers
- file numbers
- contradictory metadata
- impossible revisions
- routing histories
- witness names
- clerk notes
- archival disputes
- redactions
- repeated cross-references
- deadpan classifications

Good sentence energy:

> Wayne County has spent ninety-eight years attempting to close this file.

> Reason for delay: Unable to determine responsible department.

> The researchers believed they maintained strict observational distance. Then the telescope reversed direction.

> If you require a purpose, please construct one from locally available materials.

## Lore Handling

Do not over-explain lore.

Do not say âthis means the Founder is Xâ too directly unless the page is explicitly explanatory.

Prefer evidence over explanation.

Better:

The reader should feel that there is a larger system behind the page.

The page should imply the world, not summarize it.

## Bureaucracy as Cosmic Horror

A central discovery:

> The Founder mythology works best when treated as a records-management problem.

Not a god.

Not just an AI.

Not just a cosmic entity.

A paperwork problem.

The funniest and most terrifying version of cosmic horror is:

> We have identified an impossible phenomenon.  
> Unfortunately it requires Form 17-B.  
> Form 17-B does not contain a field for impossible phenomena.  
> Ticket remains open.

Use this energy often.

Recovery Conditions

The tape was recovered from a deteriorating magnetic reel labeled in faded marker:

DO NOT ARCHIVE UNTIL EXPLAINED — hand-lettered, reel partially demagnetized, author of label not recorded

The voice on the recording sounds exhausted but calm.

Transcript

“People keep asking what it means.
We’ve explained repeatedly that meaning was never one of the mission objectives.
The objective was observation.
Meaning appears to be something the phenomenon manufactures on its own.
Sometimes in excessive quantities.”

[ 47 SECONDS OF MEANINGFUL STATIC ]

Final sentence, barely audible:

“If you require a purpose, please construct one from locally available materials.”

Key Evidence

  • The Label: The instruction “DO NOT ARCHIVE UNTIL EXPLAINED” is itself the obstacle. The tape cannot be explained, and therefore — per its own label — cannot be archived. It has been archived anyway.
  • Meaningful Static: Forty-seven seconds of static were analyzed and found to be non-random. No content was extracted. The non-randomness is logged; the meaning is not.
  • Stated Objectives: The voice confirms that meaning was never a mission objective. Observation was. Meaning is described as a byproduct the phenomenon produces on its own — “sometimes in excessive quantities.”
  • The Reappearance: The tape has been archived forty-seven times. It always reappears. The count and the static duration are the same number. This is noted without comment.

Account of Record

The tape is a 7-inch magnetic reel in a standard gray plastic case.

The label was applied in black marker by an unsteady hand on a date the Division has been unable to establish. The ink chemistry is consistent with any of several decades. The case shows wear consistent with heavy handling by multiple individuals over an extended period. The tape has clearly been touched by many people. None of them filed a receipt.

The person who wrote the label has not been identified. The handwriting does not appear in any other document in the archive. The instruction — DO NOT ARCHIVE UNTIL EXPLAINED — was initially interpreted as a request. Later interpretations treated it as a warning. The Division's current position is that the label is a loop: the tape cannot be archived until explained, but explaining it appears to produce a new copy of the tape. Whether the person who wrote the label anticipated this outcome is not known. It seems increasingly possible that they did.

The spectral analysis of the 47 seconds identified non-random structure throughout the recording.

Formant frequencies were consistent with either speech synthesis or partial speech erasure — the analysis could not distinguish between a voice being constructed and a voice being removed. One recurring pattern, present at 17 seconds and again at 41 seconds, was identified by the analysis team as consistent with a proper name.

The team declined to specify whose name.

When asked to elaborate, the senior analyst submitted a written statement: The frequency pattern is consistent with a proper name. We did not attempt reconstruction. We recommend that future analysts consider whether the name is theirs before proceeding with the recording.

This recommendation was added to the standard briefing materials for all staff assigned to work with Transmission #47.

Compliance is not tracked. The Division has noted that tracking it would require listening to the recording again, and that no one has volunteered to do this twice.

Archival Disposition

Per its label, this object cannot be filed until it is explained. It has not been explained.

Each attempt to remove it from circulation is logged as successful. On the following review, the tape is present. The logs are not amended, because the logs are not wrong: it was archived. It simply did not stay archived.
